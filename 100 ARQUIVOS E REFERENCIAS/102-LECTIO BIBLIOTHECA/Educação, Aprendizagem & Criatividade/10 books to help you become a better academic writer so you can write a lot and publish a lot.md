10 books to help you become a better academic writer so you can write a lot and publish a lot:  
  
1\. Academic Writing as if the Reader Matters by Leonard Cassuto  
  
Practical tips on how to make your academic writing more engaging and readable. Examples from the arts and sciences.  
  
2\. The Clockwork Muse by Eviatar Zerubavel  
  
Helpful advice on how to organize your writing process in terms of time.  
  
A-Time: for writing new material (deep work)  
B-Time: for shallow work like compiling bibliography, etc.  
C-Time: for house chores  
  
3\. Write Your Dissertation in Fifteen Minutes a Day by Joan Bolker  
  
Excellent tips on how and why you should write zero drafts.  
  
Teaches you how to understand different stages of the writing process from ideation to drafting to revision.  
  
4\. How to Write a Lot by Paul Silvia  
  
Teaches you how to build a sustainable writing schedule.  
  
Also contains tips on how to start a writing group with your friends and colleagues.  
  
5\. How to Write a Thesis by Umberto Eco  
  
Witty and hilarious guide to researching and drafting a thesis with practical advice like avoid writing long sentences; Proust could write long sentences, you are not Proust.  
  
6\. The Elements of Academic Style by Eric Hayot  
  
Primarily meant for advanced grad students and faculty in the humanities.  
  
Contains wonderful advice on the mechanics of academic prose and how to hone your scholarly voice.  
  
7\. They Say, I Say by Gerald Graff and Cathy Birkenstein  
  
Practical tips on rhetoric and argumentation.  
  
Will teach you how to foreground your argument while situating it with respect to existing scholarship.  
  
8\. Make Your Manuscript Work by Laura Portwood-Stacer  
  
Teaches you how to do developmental editing on your manuscript.  
  
Tips on assessing and refining your argument, evidence, structure, and style.  
  
9\. Writing Your Journal Article in Twelve Weeks by Wendy Laura Belcher  
  
Excellent guide and workbook with exercises on how to write research papers.  
  
If you follow it you will have written a journal article in twelve weeks.  
  
10\. Writing with Pleasure by Helen Sword  
  
Teaches you how to make the writing pleasurable instead of a painful chore.
