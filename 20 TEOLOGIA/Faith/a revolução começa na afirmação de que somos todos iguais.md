‘Man is something infinitely greater than man’, said [[Pascal]]. No earth-bound conditionings could satisfy or define humanity.

Amarelo destaque | Local: 1,477 Nota | Local: 1,477

It could equally well be said that God is humanity’s place, and that this is more important than any facts of biology, sociology or psychology precisely because it is the opening through which God comes to dwell in humanity, and through which humanity itself can ascend to God. This is the only answer to totalitarian ideologies, that is, to ‘social sciences’, when they are tempted to become all-embracing and reductionist.
